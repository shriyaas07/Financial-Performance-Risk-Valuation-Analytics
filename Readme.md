# Reliance Industries — Financial Performance & Valuation Engine
End-to-End Financial Analytics, Valuation & Investment Dashboard
A complete financial analysis and valuation framework built using Python, Power BI, and financial modeling techniques.

# Project Overview
This project presents a full-scale financial analysis and valuation of Reliance Industries Ltd using:
- Financial statements
- Growth analysis
- Ratio diagnostics
- Cash flow modeling
- Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation

# Power BI executive dashboard
The goal is to demonstrate how raw financial statements can be transformed into investment-grade insights using analytics and financial modeling. This project simulates how equity research, investment banking, and corporate finance teams analyze companies.

# Tools & Technologies
Category	Tools
Data Processing	Python, Pandas, NumPy
Financial Modeling	DCF, Growth Models, Ratio Analysis
Visualization	Power BI
Documentation	Jupyter Notebook, Excel
Data Source	Company Financial Statements

# What This Project Covers
1. Financial Data Engineering
- Income Statement
- Balance Sheet
- Cash Flow Statement
All cleaned, normalized, and structured in Python.

2. Profitability & Efficiency Analysis
- Calculated metrics include:
- Gross Margin
- Operating Margin
- Net Margin
- ROA (Return on Assets)
- ROE (Return on Equity)
- Working Capital Ratio
- Debt to Equity
- Cash Flow Margins
These reveal: How efficiently Reliance converts revenue into profit and shareholder value.

3. Growth Analysis
- Using historical financials:
- Revenue Growth
- Net Income Growth
- Free Cash Flow Growth
- CAGR calculations

This shows:
Whether the company is expanding sustainably or slowing down.

4.Cash Flow & Quality of Earnings

We analyzed: Operating Cash Flow, Free Cash Flow, Net Income vs Cash
This answers: Are reported profits backed by real cash?

5. Valuation Engine (DCF)
A full Discounted Cash Flow model was built:
- Free Cash Flow forecasting
- Growth rate estimation
- Risk-adjusted discounting
- Terminal Value
- Enterprise Value
- Equity Value
- Intrinsic Share Price
This provides:
A fair value estimate based on future cash-generating ability.

# Power BI Dashboard

A 5-page executive dashboard was built using Power BI.

Pages include:

Page 1 — Financial Snapshot (Executive View)

This page provides a high-level overview of the company’s financial health.

It answers:
- How large is the company?
- Is it profitable?
- Is it financially strong?
Includes: Revenue, EBITDA, Net Income, Free Cash Flow (TTM, ROE and Debt-to-Equity and 5-year financial trends
This is the CEO / investor landing page.

Page 2 — Profitability & Margins

This page analyzes how efficiently Reliance converts revenue into profit.

It answers:
- Are margins improving or declining?
- Is cost control effective?
Includes: Gross Margin, Operating Margin, Net Margin, Operating Income vs Net Income
This is used to evaluate business quality and pricing power.

Page 3 — Balance Sheet Strength
This page focuses on capital structure and financial stability.
It answers:
- Is the company more debt-funded or equity-funded?
- Is leverage increasing?
Includes: Equity vs Total Liabilities (stacked bar), Capital structure over time
This is a credit-risk and stability view.

Page 4 — Cash Flow Quality

This page evaluates how real and sustainable profits are.

It answers:
- Are profits converting into cash?
- Is the business self-funding?
Includes: Net Income vs Free Cash Flow, Operating Cash Flow trends
This is used to detect earnings quality and cash health.

Page 5 — Valuation & Investment View

This is the most decision-oriented page.
It answers:
- What is the intrinsic value of Reliance?
- Is the stock undervalued or overvalued?
Includes: DCF-based Intrinsic Value, Enterprise Value, Equity Value, Valuation summary visuals
This page converts financial data into an investment decision.

# Key Insights
- Reliance shows strong revenue scale but moderate margin pressure
- Cash flows remain positive, supporting business sustainability
- Valuation indicates whether the stock is under- or over-valued based on fundamentals
- The company’s capital structure shows how much is funded by debt vs equity
